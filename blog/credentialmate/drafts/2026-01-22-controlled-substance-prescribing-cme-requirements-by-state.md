# Controlled Substance Prescribing CME Requirements by State

## Introduction

Controlled substance prescribing CME has become one of the most common mandatory topic requirements across states. In response to the opioid crisis, most states now require physicians who prescribe controlled substances to complete specific education on pain management, opioid prescribing, and addiction recognition. This guide outlines these requirements by state.

## Why Controlled Substance CME?

### Regulatory Response

| Driving Factor | State Response |
|----------------|----------------|
| Opioid epidemic | Mandatory education |
| Prescribing patterns | CME requirements |
| Addiction awareness | Training mandates |
| PDMP usage | Education on tools |

### Who Must Complete

| Typically Required | Typically Exempt |
|-------------------|------------------|
| DEA-registered physicians | Non-prescribers |
| Pain management prescribers | Administrative physicians |
| Primary care | Sometimes pathologists |
| Emergency medicine | Sometimes radiologists |

## State Requirements Overview

### States with Specific Requirements

| State | Requirement | Hours |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Florida | Controlled substance prescribing | 2 per cycle |
| Texas | Opioid-related content | Varies |
| California | Pain management for DEA registrants | Required |
| Michigan | Pain and symptom management | 3 per cycle |
| Washington | Opioid prescribing | Required |
| Ohio | Prescriber training | Required |
| Pennsylvania | Pain management | Required |

### Content Typically Covered

| Topic | Inclusion |
|-------|-----------|
| Safe prescribing practices | Always |
| PDMP usage | Almost always |
| Addiction recognition | Usually |
| Alternative treatments | Often |
| Tapering protocols | Common |
| State regulations | Usually |

### Meeting Requirements

## Multi-State Considerations

If licensed in multiple states with opioid requirements:
- Complete most comprehensive course
- Verify meets each state's specific requirements
- Document compliance for each state

## Key Takeaways

- **Most states require** controlled substance CME for prescribers
- **Check your specific state** for exact requirements
- **Complete early** in renewal cycle
- **Verify approval** before completing course
- **Document separately** from general CME
