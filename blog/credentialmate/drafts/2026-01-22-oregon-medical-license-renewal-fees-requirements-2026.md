# Oregon Medical License Renewal: Fees and Requirements 2026

## Introduction

Oregon physicians maintain their medical licenses through the Oregon Medical Board (OMB). With a biennial renewal cycle and 60 CME hours required per cycle, Oregon maintains moderate requirements compared to other states. The Pacific Northwest state's participation in the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact (IMLC) makes it convenient for multi-state practitioners, particularly those also practicing in Washington.

This guide covers everything Oregon physicians need to know about medical license renewal in 2026, including current fees, CME requirements, and the renewal process.

## Online Renewal Process

### Using OMB Online Portal

1. **Access Portal**: Visit oregon.gov/omb
2. **Log In**: Use your credentials
3. **Select Renewal**: Choose license renewal
4. **Complete Application**:
   - Verify personal information
   - Update practice information
   - Answer disclosure questions
   - Certify CME completion (60 hours)
   - Confirm mandatory topics completed
5. **Pay Fee**: Submit payment electronically
6. **Confirmation**: Save receipt

## Consequences of Non-Renewal

### Lapsed License

| Status | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| Expired (grace period) | Late fees apply |
| Practicing without license | Violation of ORS 677 |
| Extended lapse | Reinstatement required |

### Legal Consequences

Under Oregon law, practicing medicine without a valid license:
- Is a Class C felony (ORS 677.080)
- Subject to civil penalties
- Results in board disciplinary action
- May affect licensing in other states

### Reinstatement

For lapsed licenses:
1. Apply to OMB for reinstatement
2. Pay applicable fees
3. Demonstrate CME compliance
4. Complete any required evaluations
5. Await board approval

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to renew a medical license in Oregon?

The biennial renewal fee is approximately $350-400. Late fees apply if renewal is submitted after expiration. Reinstatement after lapse requires additional fees and may involve additional requirements.

### How many CME hours does Oregon require?

Oregon requires 60 hours of CME per biennial (2-year) renewal period. This equals 30 hours per year, placing Oregon in the moderate range nationally.

### What is the pain management CME requirement?

Oregon requires physicians who prescribe controlled substances to complete pain management continuing education. Check with the Oregon Medical Board for current specific hour requirements and approved content.

### When does my Oregon medical license expire?

Oregon medical licenses expire based on your original license issuance date. Check your license document or the OMB online verification system to confirm your specific expiration date.

### Does Oregon participate in the IMLC?

Yes. Oregon is a participating member of the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact. Physicians can use Oregon as their state of principal license or obtain an Oregon license through the compact's expedited process.
