# Arizona Medical License Renewal: Fees and Requirements 2026

## Introduction

Arizona physicians maintain their medical licenses through the Arizona Medical Board (AMB). With biennial renewal, 40 CME hours required, and a growing healthcare market in the Southwest, Arizona represents an important state for physicians. The state's participation in the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact (IMLC) and its general business-friendly regulatory environment make it attractive for multi-state practitioners.

This guide covers everything Arizona physicians need to know about medical license renewal in 2026, including current fees, CME requirements, and the renewal process.

## Renewal Timeline

### Biennial Cycle

Arizona uses a biennial (2-year) renewal cycle:

| Event | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| Renewal cycle | Every 2 years |
| Expiration | Based on birth date |
| Renewal window | Opens ~90 days before expiration |
| Deadline | Birth month, every 2 years |
| Grace period | Limited—check with AMB |

### Birth Month Renewal

Arizona licenses expire based on your birth month:
- If born in March, license expires March 31 of renewal year
- Renewal years alternate (even/odd based on original issuance)

## Online Renewal Process

### Using AMB Online Portal

1. **Access Portal**: Visit azmd.gov
2. **Log In**: Use your AMB account credentials
3. **Select Renewal**: Choose license renewal
4. **Complete Application**:
   - Verify personal information
   - Update practice location
   - Answer disclosure questions (malpractice, discipline, criminal)
   - Certify CME completion (40 hours)
5. **Pay Fee**: Submit payment electronically
6. **Confirmation**: Save receipt and confirmation

### License Verification

Verify Arizona physician licenses at:
https://azbomprod.azmd.gov/GLSPublic/DesktopDefault.aspx

## Consequences of Non-Renewal

### Lapsed License

| Status | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| Expired (grace period) | Late fee applies |
| Lapsed beyond grace | Cannot practice |
| Practicing without license | Violation of A.R.S. § 32-1401 |

### Legal Consequences

Under Arizona law, practicing medicine without a valid license:
- Is a Class 6 felony (A.R.S. § 32-1401)
- Subjects physician to criminal prosecution
- Results in civil liability
- May lead to permanent licensing issues

### Reinstatement

For lapsed licenses:
1. Apply for reinstatement
2. Pay reinstatement fee plus back fees
3. Demonstrate CME compliance
4. Complete background check
5. Await board review

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to renew a medical license in Arizona?

The biennial renewal fee is approximately $500. A late fee of $100 applies if renewal is submitted during the grace period. Reinstatement after lapse requires additional fees.

### How many CME hours does Arizona require?

Arizona requires 40 hours of CME per biennial (2-year) renewal period. This averages to 20 hours per year, consistent with states like Georgia and Florida.

### When does my Arizona medical license expire?

Arizona medical licenses expire in your birth month. If your birthday is in June, your license expires June 30 of your renewal year. Renewal years are every 2 years based on your original license issuance.

### Does Arizona participate in the IMLC?

Yes. Arizona is a participating member of the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact. Physicians can use Arizona as their state of principal license or obtain an Arizona license through the compact's expedited process.

### What happens if I practice with an expired Arizona license?

Practicing medicine without a valid license is a Class 6 felony under Arizona law (A.R.S. § 32-1401). This can result in criminal prosecution, civil liability, and permanent impacts on your ability to obtain licensure.
