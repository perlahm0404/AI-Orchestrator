# Washington Medical License Renewal: Fees and Requirements 2026

## Introduction

Washington State physicians maintain their medical licenses through the Washington Medical Commission (WMC). With a quadrennial (4-year) renewal cycle and 200 hours of CME required per cycle, Washington has one of the longest renewal periods but also one of the highest total CME requirements in the nation. The state's unique structure requires careful planning.

This guide covers everything Washington physicians need to know about medical license renewal in 2026, including current fees, CME requirements, and the renewal process.

## Washington Medical License Overview

### Regulatory Authority

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Regulatory Body | Washington Medical Commission (WMC) |
| Authority | RCW 18.71 (Medical Practice Act) |
| Administrative Rules | WAC 246-919 |
| Website | wmc.wa.gov |
| Parent Agency | Department of Health |

### License Types

| License Type | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| Full License (MD) | Unrestricted practice authority |
| Limited License | Specific settings or supervision |
| Retired Active License | Volunteer/limited practice |
| Osteopathic License | Governed by OBMP (separate board) |

**Note**: Osteopathic physicians (DOs) in Washington are licensed by the Osteopathic Medical Board of Physicians (OBMP), not the WMC.

Washington requires **200 hours of CME per 4-year cycle**—the highest total among U.S. states, though the annual average (50 hours) matches other high-requirement states.

## Renewal Timeline

### Quadrennial Cycle

Washington's unique 4-year renewal cycle:

| Event | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| Renewal cycle | Every 4 years |
| Expiration | Based on original issuance |
| Renewal window | Opens prior to expiration |
| Renewal notice | Sent ~90 days before |
| Grace period | Check with WMC |

### Birth Date Renewal

Washington licenses may expire based on your birthday. Verify your specific expiration date with WMC.

## Online Renewal Process

### Using WMC Online Portal

1. **Access Portal**: Visit wmc.wa.gov
2. **Log In**: Use your credentials
3. **Select Renewal**: Choose license renewal
4. **Complete Application**:
   - Verify personal information
   - Update practice information
   - Answer disclosure questions
   - Certify CME completion (200 hours)
   - Confirm mandatory topics completed
5. **Pay Fee**: Submit payment electronically
6. **Confirmation**: Save receipt

### License Verification

Verify Washington physician licenses at:
https://fortress.wa.gov/doh/providercredentialsearch/

## Consequences of Non-Renewal

### Lapsed License

| Status | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| Expired | Cannot practice in Washington |
| Practicing without license | Violation of RCW 18.71 |
| Extended lapse | Reinstatement required |

### Legal Consequences

Under Washington law, practicing medicine without a valid license:
- Is a gross misdemeanor or felony (depending on circumstances)
- Subject to civil penalties
- Results in board disciplinary action
- May affect licensing in other states

### Reinstatement

For lapsed licenses:
1. Apply to WMC for reinstatement
2. Pay applicable fees
3. Demonstrate CME compliance
4. Complete any required evaluations
5. Await commission approval

## Washington-Specific Requirements

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often do I need to renew my Washington medical license?

Washington uses a quadrennial (4-year) renewal cycle—the longest cycle in the United States. Your license expires every 4 years based on your original issuance date or birthday.

### How many CME hours does Washington require?

Washington requires 200 hours of CME per 4-year renewal cycle. This equals 50 hours annually and is the highest total requirement in the nation, though the annual average matches other high-requirement states like Massachusetts and Pennsylvania.

### What is the suicide prevention training requirement?

Washington mandates 6 hours of suicide assessment, treatment, and management training per renewal cycle. This is included in the 200-hour total but must be specifically documented. Approved courses are available through various providers.

### How much does it cost to renew a medical license in Washington?

The quadrennial renewal fee is approximately $700-800. When divided over 4 years, this equals roughly $175-200 annually. Late fees apply if renewal is submitted after expiration.

### Does Washington participate in the IMLC?

Yes. Washington is a participating member of the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact. Physicians can use Washington as their state of principal license or obtain a Washington license through the compact's expedited process.
