# Medical License Renewal Late Fees by State: Complete Guide

## Introduction

Missing your medical license renewal deadline doesn't just mean paperwork delays—it means late fees that can add hundreds of dollars to your renewal cost. And in states without grace periods, it may mean you can't practice at all until your renewal is processed.

Late fees vary dramatically by state, from modest flat fees to percentage-based penalties that can exceed $600. Understanding your state's late fee structure—and more importantly, avoiding these fees entirely—is essential for every practicing physician. This comprehensive guide covers medical license renewal late fees by state for 2026.

## Late Fee Structures: How States Calculate Penalties

### Types of Late Fee Structures

| Structure | How It Works | Example |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| Flat fee | Fixed amount regardless of base fee | Oregon: $195 flat |
| Percentage | Percent of renewal fee | California: 10-50% |
| Tiered | Increasing penalty over time | Many states |
| Daily | Per-day penalty | Rare |

### States with Percentage-Based Late Fees

| State | Base Renewal | Late Fee | Maximum Penalty |
|-------|--------------|----------|-----------------|
| California | $1,206 | 10% (0-30 days), 50% (90+ days) | $603 |
| Texas | ~$491 | Varies by time | Varies |
| Florida | $360 | Varies | Varies |

### States with Flat Late Fees

| State | Base Renewal | Late Fee |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| Oregon | Varies | $195 |
| Washington | Varies | Fixed per rules |

## State-by-State Late Fee Reference

### California

**Base Fee**: $1,206 (biennial)

| Time Late | Fee Structure | Amount |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| 0-30 days | 10% of renewal | $120.60 |
| 31-90 days | 10% of renewal | $120.60 |
| 90+ days | 50% of renewal | $603.00 |

**Authority**: California Code of Regulations Section 1365

**Grace Period**: None—license expires at midnight on expiration date

**Total worst case**: $1,206 + $603 = **$1,809**

### Texas

**Base Fee**: ~$491 (biennial)

| Time Late | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| 0-30 days (grace) | Standard fee + late penalty |
| 30+ days | Enhanced late fees |
| Extended lapse | May require additional documentation |

**Grace Period**: 30 days (but penalties still apply)

### Florida

**Grace Period**: 30 days

### New York

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the highest medical license late fee?

California has among the highest late fees, reaching 50% of the renewal fee ($603 on a $1,206 renewal) for renewals more than 90 days late. Some states may require payment of back fees for extended lapses, potentially exceeding this amount.

### Can I practice during my state's grace period?

It depends on the state. Some states allow practice during grace periods; others do not. Even when allowed, you may face late fees, and some employers and hospitals don't recognize grace periods. Verify your specific state's rules.

### Which states have no grace period for medical license renewal?

California, Maryland, and New York have no grace period. Your license expires at midnight on the expiration date, and practicing after that time is unlicensed practice—a criminal offense.

### Can late fees be waived?

Some state boards have authority to waive late fees for circumstances like military deployment, medical emergencies, or natural disasters. Submit a written request with documentation. Waivers are not guaranteed and policies vary by state.

### How do I find out my state's specific late fee?

Check your state medical board's website under "fees" or "renewal information." The FSMB also maintains contact information for all state boards at fsmb.org/contact-a-state-medical-board.
