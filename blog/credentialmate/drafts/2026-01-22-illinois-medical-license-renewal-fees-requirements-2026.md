### Using IDFPR Online Services

2. **Log In**: Create or access your account
3. **Select Renewal**: Choose physician license renewal
4. **Complete Application**:
   - Verify personal information
   - Update address if needed
   - Answer disclosure questions
   - Certify CME completion

## Consequences of Non-Renewal

### Lapsed License

| Status | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| Expired | Cannot practice in Illinois |
| Practicing without license | Violation of Medical Practice Act |
| Extended lapse | Reinstatement required |

### Medical Practice Act Violations

Under 225 ILCS 60, practicing without a valid license may result in:
- Civil penalties
- Criminal charges (Class A misdemeanor or felony)
- Board disciplinary action
- Inability to collect fees for services

### Reinstatement Process

If your license lapses:
1. Apply for reinstatement
2. Pay reinstatement fee plus back fees
3. Complete any additional CME requirements
4. Pass background check (if required)
5. Wait for board approval

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often do I need to renew my Illinois medical license?

Illinois medical licenses renew every 3 years (triennially). Your specific expiration date depends on when your license was originally issued. Check IDFPR's online verification system to confirm your expiration date.

### How many CME hours are required for Illinois license renewal?

Illinois requires 150 hours of CME per triennial (3-year) renewal period. This averages to 50 hours per year, placing Illinois among the higher-requirement states when annualized.

### What happens if I don't renew my Illinois medical license on time?

Practicing with an expired license violates the Illinois Medical Practice Act (225 ILCS 60). Consequences may include civil penalties, criminal charges, disciplinary action, and the need to go through reinstatement with additional fees.

### Does Illinois participate in the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact?

Yes. Illinois is a participating member of the IMLC, allowing eligible physicians to obtain Illinois licenses through the expedited compact process. Physicians can also use an Illinois license as their state of principal license for IMLC applications.

### Can I put my Illinois medical license on inactive status?

Yes. Illinois offers inactive status for physicians not actively practicing in the state. Contact IDFPR for specific requirements and reduced fees. Reactivating an inactive license typically requires demonstrating current competency and CME compliance.
