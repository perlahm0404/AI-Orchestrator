# Florida Physician License Renewal: Fees and CME Requirements

## Introduction

Florida physicians must renew their medical license biennially through the Florida Board of Medicine. As the third most populous state with a significant retiree population and growing healthcare needs, Florida maintains specific CME requirements including several mandatory topics that physicians must complete before renewal.

Understanding Florida physician license renewal requirements is critical—the state requires not just general CME hours but specific courses in HIV/AIDS, medical errors prevention, domestic violence, and human trafficking. This guide covers everything you need to know about Florida medical license renewal in 2026, including current fees, detailed CME requirements, and the renewal process.

## Florida Medical License Renewal Fees (as of January 2026)

The Florida Board of Medicine sets renewal fees under Florida Administrative Code Chapter 64B8.

| Fee Type | Amount | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Biennial License Renewal (Active) | $360 | Standard renewal |
| Inactive License Renewal | $180 | 50% of active fee |
| Late Renewal Fee | Varies | Additional penalty |
| Reactivation Fee | $100 | Plus back fees |

### Fee Payment Details

- **Payment Methods**: Credit card, debit card, or electronic check
- **Processing Time**: 7-10 business days for online renewals
- **Receipt**: Save confirmation for your records

*Source: [Florida Board of Medicine Fee Schedule](https://flboardofmedicine.gov/licensing/fees/)*
*Last verified: January 22, 2026*

## Florida CME Requirements

Florida has specific continuing medical education requirements established under Florida Administrative Code 64B8-13.005. Unlike some states with simple hour requirements, Florida mandates specific topics that must be completed.

### Basic CME Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Total Hours | 40 hours per biennial period |
| Credit Type | AMA PRA Category 1 or equivalent |
| Mandatory Topics | See detailed requirements below |
| Reporting | Self-certification at renewal |

### Mandatory CME Topics

Florida requires the following specific courses as part of your 40-hour requirement:

| Topic | Hours Required | Frequency | Authority |
|-------|----------------|-----------|-----------|
| HIV/AIDS | 2 hours | Every renewal | FAC 64B8-13.005 |
| Prevention of Medical Errors | 2 hours | Every renewal | FAC 64B8-13.005 |
| Domestic Violence | 2 hours | Every renewal | FAC 64B8-13.005 |
| Human Trafficking | 1 hour | Every renewal | FAC 64B8-13.005 |
| Laws and Rules | 2 hours | One-time for initial renewal | FAC 64B8-13.005 |
| Prescribing Controlled Substances | 2 hours | If prescribing controlled substances | FAC 64B8-13.005 |

### HIV/AIDS Requirement (2 Hours)

All Florida physicians must complete a Board-approved HIV/AIDS course covering:

- Epidemiology and transmission
- Clinical management
- Prevention counseling
- Legal and ethical issues
- Florida-specific reporting requirements

### Prevention of Medical Errors (2 Hours)

Required topics include:

- Root cause analysis
- Error reduction strategies
- Patient safety culture
- Reporting systems
- Florida-specific requirements

### Domestic Violence (2 Hours)

Course content must address:

- Screening and identification
- Documentation requirements
- Safety planning
- Referral resources
- Legal reporting obligations

### Human Trafficking (1 Hour)

Required for recognition and response:

- Warning signs and indicators
- Victim-centered approach
- Mandatory reporting requirements
- Available resources

### Controlled Substances Prescribing (2 Hours)

If you prescribe controlled substances, you must complete:

- Florida PDMP requirements
- Opioid prescribing guidelines
- Risk assessment and mitigation
- Addiction recognition

## Florida's 30-Day Grace Period

Florida provides a **30-day grace period** after your license expiration date. During this period:

- You may continue to practice
- Late fees may apply
- Renewal must be completed within 30 days
- After 30 days, license becomes delinquent

### Important Grace Period Limitations

While Florida allows a 30-day grace period, there are critical considerations:

1. **Hospital Privileges**: Many hospitals do not recognize grace periods and require active licensure
2. **Insurance Coverage**: Verify your malpractice policy covers practice during grace period
3. **Employment Contracts**: Review your contract for specific license requirements
4. **Medicare/Medicaid**: Federal programs may have different requirements

## Online License Renewal Process

### Step-by-Step Instructions

1. **Access MQA Portal**: Visit the Florida Department of Health Medical Quality Assurance portal
2. **Log In**: Use your existing credentials or create an account
3. **Select Renewal**: Choose "Renew License" from your dashboard
4. **Complete Application**: Answer all required questions including:
   - Practice address verification
   - Disciplinary disclosure
   - CME compliance certification
5. **Pay Fee**: Submit $360 via electronic payment
6. **Receive Confirmation**: Save and print your receipt

### License Verification

Verify your license status or look up other physicians at:
[Florida MQA License Verification](https://mqa-internet.doh.state.fl.us/MQASearchServices/Home)

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

### Practicing Without Valid License

Florida Statutes Chapter 458 prohibits practicing medicine without a valid license:

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| First offense | Third-degree felony |
| Subsequent | Enhanced penalties |
| Civil liability | Uninsured practice exposure |
| Board action | Disciplinary proceedings |

### CME Non-Compliance

Failure to complete required CME may result in:

- License renewal denial
- Formal investigation
- Administrative penalties
- Public disciplinary action
- NPDB reporting

### Incomplete Mandatory Topics

Renewing without completing all mandatory topics (HIV/AIDS, medical errors, domestic violence, human trafficking) will result in:

- Application rejection
- Delayed renewal
- Potential late fees
- Required completion before license activation

## Inactive License Option

Florida offers an inactive license status for physicians not currently practicing:

| Feature | Active License | Inactive License |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| Renewal Fee | $360 | $180 |
| CME Required | 40 hours | None |
| Practice Allowed | Yes | No |
| Reactivation | N/A | $100 + current CME |

### When to Consider Inactive Status

- Extended leave from practice
- Retirement from clinical work
- Administrative-only roles
- Out-of-state relocation (temporary)

### Reactivating an Inactive License

To return to active status:

1. Complete current CME requirements (40 hours with mandatory topics)
2. Submit reactivation application
3. Pay reactivation fee ($100) plus active renewal fee ($360)
4. Pass any required competency evaluation (if inactive >5 years)

## Key Takeaways

- **Biennial renewal fee is $360** for active license

- **Controlled substances prescribers** need additional 2 hours
- **30-day grace period** available but has limitations
- **Inactive status** available at reduced fee with no CME requirement

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to renew a medical license in Florida?

The biennial renewal fee for an active Florida medical license is $360. Inactive licenses renew for $180. Late fees and reactivation fees may apply depending on your circumstances.

### What CME is required for Florida physician license renewal?

Florida requires 40 hours of CME per biennial period, including mandatory courses: 2 hours HIV/AIDS, 2 hours prevention of medical errors, 2 hours domestic violence, 1 hour human trafficking, and 2 hours controlled substances prescribing (if applicable).

### Does Florida have a grace period for medical license renewal?

Yes, Florida provides a 30-day grace period after license expiration during which you may continue to practice. However, late fees may apply, and some employers, hospitals, and insurance companies may not recognize the grace period.

### What happens if I don't complete all mandatory CME topics in Florida?

Your renewal application will be rejected if you haven't completed all mandatory topics. You must complete HIV/AIDS, medical errors, domestic violence, and human trafficking courses before your license can be renewed.

### Can I put my Florida medical license on inactive status?

Yes, Florida offers inactive status for physicians not currently practicing. Inactive licenses renew for $180 (half the active fee) and require no CME. However, you cannot practice medicine on an inactive license and must complete reactivation requirements to return to active status.
