# Georgia Medical License Renewal: Fees and Requirements 2026

## Introduction

Georgia physicians maintain their medical licenses through the Georgia Composite Medical Board (GCMB). With biennial renewal, 40 hours of CME required, and a moderate fee structure, Georgia's licensing requirements fall in the middle range nationally. The state's growing healthcare sector and participation in the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact make Georgia an important state for many physicians.

This guide covers everything Georgia physicians need to know about medical license renewal in 2026, including current fees, CME requirements, and the renewal process.

## Online Renewal Process

### Using Georgia's Online System

1. **Access Portal**: Visit Georgia's professional licensing portal
2. **Log In**: Use your credentials
3. **Select License**: Choose medical license renewal
4. **Complete Application**:
   - Verify personal information
   - Update address and practice information
   - Answer disclosure questions
   - Certify CME completion (40 hours)
5. **Pay Fee**: Submit $350 electronically
6. **Confirmation**: Save receipt

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to renew a medical license in Georgia?

The biennial renewal fee is $350 for an active medical license. Late fees apply if renewal is submitted after expiration. Reinstatement after an extended lapse requires additional fees.

### How many CME hours does Georgia require?

Georgia requires 40 hours of CME per biennial (2-year) renewal period. This is equivalent to 20 hours per year, which is lower than many states and consistent with Florida's requirements.

### When do Georgia medical licenses expire?

Georgia medical licenses expire based on your original license issuance date. Check the GCMB website or your license document for your specific expiration date. Renewal windows typically open 60-90 days before expiration.

### Does Georgia participate in the IMLC?

Yes. Georgia is a full participating member of the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact. Physicians can use Georgia as their state of principal license (SPL) for IMLC applications or obtain a Georgia license through the compact.

### What happens if my Georgia medical license expires?

Practicing with an expired license violates O.C.G.A. ยง 43-34-37 and can result in criminal penalties, civil fines, and board disciplinary action. If your license lapses, you must apply for reinstatement with additional fees and demonstrate CME compliance.
