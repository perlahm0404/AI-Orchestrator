# Free CME Resources for Physicians: 2026 Complete Guide

## Introduction

CME requirements can be expensive. With states requiring anywhere from 24 to 200 hours per renewal cycle, the cost of continuing medical education adds up quickly. Fortunately, high-quality free CME is widely available from reputable sources—often offering the same AMA PRA Category 1 credits as paid courses.

This guide compiles the best free CME resources for physicians in 2026, covering general medicine, specialty-specific content, and mandatory topics required by various states.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is free CME as good as paid CME?

Free CME from reputable providers (Medscape, CDC, medical schools) is high-quality and offers the same AMA PRA Category 1 credits as paid courses. However, free CME is typically online-only and may lack the depth of specialized paid programs.

### Can I complete all my CME requirements for free?

Yes, in most states. Medscape alone offers enough free Category 1 credits to satisfy requirements in most states. However, some mandatory topics may have limited free options.

### How do I know if free CME is legitimate?

Verify the provider is ACCME-accredited and the activity offers AMA PRA Category 1 Credit. Check that your certificate includes the proper credit statement.

### Does Medscape CME count for license renewal?

Yes. Medscape is accredited by ACCME and offers AMA PRA Category 1 credits, which are accepted by all state medical boards that require CME.

### Are there free CME courses for mandatory state topics?

Yes, though availability varies. Opioid prescribing, medical ethics, and many other mandatory topics are available free through Medscape, CDC, and state health departments. Check your specific state's approved provider list.
