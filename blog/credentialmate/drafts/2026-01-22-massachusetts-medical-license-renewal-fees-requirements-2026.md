# Massachusetts Medical License Renewal: Fees and Requirements 2026

## Introduction

Massachusetts physicians maintain their medical licenses through the Board of Registration in Medicine (BORIM). As home to world-renowned medical institutions and teaching hospitals, Massachusetts has a large and active physician community. The state's biennial renewal cycle and 100-hour CME requirement (among the highest nationally) reflect its emphasis on continuing education and professional standards.

This guide covers everything Massachusetts physicians need to know about medical license renewal in 2026, including current fees, CME requirements, and the renewal process.

## Renewal Timeline

### Biennial Cycle

Massachusetts uses a biennial (2-year) renewal cycle:

| Event | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| Renewal cycle | Every 2 years |
| Expiration | Based on license issuance |
| Renewal window | Opens ~60-90 days before |
| Renewal notice | Sent prior to expiration |

### Finding Your Expiration Date

2. **BORIM online**: License verification system
3. **Contact BORIM**: Direct inquiry

## Online Renewal Process

### Using Massachusetts ePLACE Portal

1. **Access Portal**: mass.gov/eplace
2. **Log In**: Use your credentials
3. **Select Renewal**: Choose physician license renewal
4. **Complete Application**:
   - Verify personal information
   - Update practice information
   - Answer disclosure questions
   - Certify CME completion (100 hours)
5. **Pay Fee**: Submit payment electronically
6. **Confirmation**: Save receipt

### Legal Consequences

Under Massachusetts law, practicing medicine without a valid license:
- Is a criminal offense
- Subject to civil penalties
- Results in board disciplinary action
- May affect future licensing nationally

### Reinstatement

For lapsed licenses:

4. Complete any required evaluations

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to renew a medical license in Massachusetts?

The biennial renewal fee is approximately $600. Late fees apply if renewal is submitted after expiration. Reinstatement after lapse requires additional fees and may involve additional requirements.

### How many CME hours does Massachusetts require?

Massachusetts requires 100 hours of CME per biennial (2-year) renewal period. This equals 50 hours per year and is among the highest requirements in the United States.

### When does my Massachusetts medical license expire?

Massachusetts medical licenses expire based on your original license issuance date. Check your license document or the BORIM online verification system to confirm your specific expiration date.

### Does Massachusetts participate in the IMLC?

Yes. Massachusetts is a participating member of the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact. Physicians can use Massachusetts as their state of principal license or obtain a Massachusetts license through the compact's expedited process.

### What happens if I don't complete 100 CME hours?

Your renewal will be denied until you complete the required hours. If you certify completion falsely, you face disciplinary action. Plan to complete 50 hours annually throughout the cycle to stay compliant.
