# North Carolina Medical License Renewal: Fees and Requirements 2026

## Introduction

North Carolina physicians renew their medical licenses through the North Carolina Medical Board (NCMB). With an annual renewal cycle, 60 CME hours over three years, and a straightforward fee structure, North Carolina maintains an active licensing framework that requires yearly attention from physicians.

This guide covers everything North Carolina physicians need to know about medical license renewal in 2026, including current fees, CME requirements, and the renewal process.

### Fee Context

North Carolina's annual renewal means:
- **Annual cost**: ~$325/year
- **Higher annual outlay** than biennial states (which spread cost over 2 years)
- **Comparison**: Similar to many states when annualized
- **Payment**: Online through NCMB portal

## CME Requirements

### Basic Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Total Hours | 60 per 3-year period |
| Annual Average | 20 hours |
| Credit Type | AMA PRA Category 1 or equivalent |
| Cycle | 3 years (rolling) |

### CME Cycle Details

North Carolina uses a **3-year rolling CME cycle**:
- Physicians must complete 60 hours over any 3-year period
- Not tied to specific calendar years
- Verified at each annual renewal
- No carryover of excess hours

## Online Renewal Process

### Using NCMB Online Portal

1. **Access Portal**: Visit ncmedboard.org
2. **Log In**: Use your physician portal credentials
3. **Select Renewal**: Choose annual license renewal
4. **Complete Application**:
   - Verify personal information
   - Update practice information
   - Answer disclosure questions
   - Certify CME compliance (60 hours over 3 years)
5. **Pay Fee**: Submit payment electronically
6. **Confirmation**: Save receipt

### Legal Consequences

Under N.C. Gen. Stat. ยง 90-18, practicing medicine without a license is:
- A Class 1 misdemeanor (first offense)
- A Class I felony (subsequent offenses)
- Subject to civil penalties and injunctions

### Reinstatement

For lapsed licenses:
1. Submit reinstatement application

3. Demonstrate CME compliance

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why does North Carolina require annual renewal instead of biennial?

North Carolina chose an annual renewal cycle to maintain more frequent contact with licensees and ensure current information. While this requires yearly attention, it allows the board to stay current with physician practice information and compliance status.

### How does the 3-year CME cycle work with annual renewal?

You must maintain 60 CME hours over any rolling 3-year period. At each annual renewal, the board looks back 3 years to verify compliance. Complete approximately 20 hours per year to stay on track consistently.

### What is the North Carolina medical license renewal fee?

The annual renewal fee is approximately $325. Because North Carolina renews annually rather than biennially, the annual outlay is higher than states that spread fees over two years, but the per-year cost is similar to many states.

### Does North Carolina participate in the IMLC?

Yes. North Carolina is a full participating member of the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact. Physicians can use North Carolina as their state of principal license or obtain a North Carolina license through the compact's expedited process.

### What happens if I don't renew my North Carolina license on time?

Practicing with an expired license violates N.C. Gen. Stat. ยง 90-18 and is a criminal offense. Late renewal incurs penalty fees, and extended lapses require formal reinstatement with additional requirements and fees.
